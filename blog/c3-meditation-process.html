Process over product

During the rebuild project there were times when I struggled getting the page to look the way I wanted it to. I spent a lot of time with dev tools and searching the internet to try to find a solution often without result. There were times when I just sat in front of my screen hoping I could will my code to work. It became very frustrating and stressful.

I soon came to realise that I had become totally obsessed with the product, and not the process.

Once I became mindful of this, I stepped back, let it go and walked away. When I returned to the project, I reached out for help both on slack from the cohort and from EDA. Suggestions soon followed and I was able to move forward again.

My approach was different this time. I focussed more on the process and applying the time management stategies we learnt last week. I became less attached to the idea of what I wanted to create.

Although I’m not entirely happy with the end result, I realise that I’m only at the beginning of this journey so I’ll be revisiting this project later on. The process of code - test - revise.

Overall, the lesson I have learnt is that if you focus on your process, the product will follow.

Search inside yourself Chapter 2

I’m very comfortable with the idea of meditation and it’s benefits. Mindfulness is something I try to practice everyday at work and at home.

I found chapter 2 interesting as it confirmed some of my thoughts around meditation and the benefits to our mental and physical well being. It served as a useful reminder to meditate more on a regular basis and to approach it with intent.

Having the process spelt out in a clear manner has helped me to tidy up my practice, building attentiveness and dealing with distractions.

Now I just have to commit to turning it into a habit.
