Learning @ EDA

Reflections on the bootcamp experience

6 April 2016

What is your take on the DBA/EDA experience?

The experience so far has been good. I like the philosophy of the academy, focussing on our soft skills and personal development alongside the technical aspects of coding.

What are your impressions?

It's not going to be easy. There's a lot of work and learning to be done but so far it has been good. The orientation exercises were a useful introduction although I didn't realise how important they were to do until I started. The resources provided are excellent and really interesting making time management difficult.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

In Shereef's Fireside chat, he described his course as being transformative for many people. I love the idea of being part of a group, learning together and contributing to the overall experience of the bootcamp cohort.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous

I am nervous about the whole thing but I guess it's expected. If the first 3 days of phase 0 are anything to go by, there will be times of extreme frustration mixed in with a great sense of acheivement when things work out. Many highs and lows. I'm excited about meeting my cohort and going on this journey together.
