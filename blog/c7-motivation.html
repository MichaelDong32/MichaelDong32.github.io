Motivation

Week 7 - nearly at the end of Phase 0. This weeks meditation asked deep questions. What motivates us to do whatever it is that we do?

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

If you live your life from the perspective achieving your higher purpose, the other two tend to follow. It can energise and excite you and it gives your work and life meaning. Unfortunately given the way we live nowadays, we don’t always take time to discover this for ourselves.

We can easily confuse passion and things which give us pleasure as what drives us forward but it’s not until we stop and ask the question why and what for that think about this. Those two elements are important but long term are not sustainable. They encourage us to grasp and accumulate.

In my own life, I am still trying to discover what my higher purpose is. I wonder if it can change. I ‘ve come to the realisation that the answer really comes from alignment of your own core principles and values with your work and community, then you are on your way.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, What are your core values? What do you stand for?

My core values are

  • Honesty
  • Empathy & Understanding
  • Compasssion
  • Kindness
  • Sustainablity
  • Personal freedom and choice
  • Community

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I like the idea of how we can build our resilience through our meditation practice. Understanding that the uncomfortable feeings associated with disappointment or difficult situations are physiological reminds us that they are only fleeting and will pass. Reinforcing with positive thoughts and anchoring with previous success builds on the idea that we can bounce back from adversity.

Adopting a forward looking, optimistic view and a willingness to accept and move on from our failures will serve us better than dwelling on them and holding us back.
